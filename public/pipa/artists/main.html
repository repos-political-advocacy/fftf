Copyright, the Internet and the Protect-IP Act

An open letter to the US Senate and House of Representatives

TO members of the United States Congress

I am writing to you as a voter in your district. I urge you to refuse to co-sponsor the House version of S. 968, the PROTECT IP Act. The PROTECT IP Act is dangerous, ineffective, and short-sighted. We expect the House version, whose introduction by Reps. Goodlatte and Smith is expected this week, to be even worse. The drafts that have been circulated to date could even put Twitter and YouTube out of business.

Even if you think you might eventually support the legislation, please decline to cosponsor it until you've had a chance to hear from the many businesses, advocacy organizations, and ordinary Americans who oppose this legislation.

Congress needs to hear from you, or this bill passes

The big corporations that own copyrights are pushing legislation to criminalize the streaming of copyrighted content. If it passed, even Justin Bieber could face five years in the slammer!

Even worse, it's going to be combined with another bill that will give the government and private corporations new powers to block Americans access to websites that have been accused of copyright violations.

The bill would criminalize posting all sorts of standard web content -- music playing in the background of videos, footage of people dancing, kids playing video games, and even precisely what Bieber did to get famous: posting video of people singing cover songs.

It looks like the bill's going to be introduced in the House of Representatives THIS WEEK -- probably TODAY.

The bill's backers are trying to line up as much support as possible. But if we act fast, we might be able to convince some lawmakers to refuse to cosponsor the bill -- the more cosponsors, the more likely it is that it will pass. Will you fill out the form below to ask your lawmakers to refuse to sponsor this insane legislation?
